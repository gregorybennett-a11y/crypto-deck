📖 How to Use This Dashboard

A plain-English guide — no crypto knowledge required.

What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is digital money that exists only on the internet — no bank or government controls it. Bitcoin (BTC) is the most well-known, like digital gold. Ethereum (ETH) is the second largest, and powers many apps and digital contracts online. Their prices change constantly based on supply, demand, and public sentiment — just like stocks.

What Does This Dashboard Do?

It tracks the current price of Bitcoin and Ethereum and uses a mathematical model to predict where the price might go over the next 7, 30, or 90 days. It pulls data from multiple sources every morning to keep predictions fresh:

  • Yahoo Finance — real-time and historical price data
  • Fear & Greed Index — measures how nervous or excited the overall crypto market is (0 = everyone is panicking, 100 = everyone is overconfident)
  • News headlines — scans crypto news sites for positive or negative coverage
  • Reddit — reads community sentiment from crypto forums

How to Read the Chart

  • The solid orange (or blue) line is the real historical price — what actually happened.
  • The dashed line after "TODAY" is the model's best guess for the future price.
  • The shaded band around the dashed line is the uncertainty range — the price will likely land somewhere inside it. A wider band means more uncertainty.
  • The dotted white line is the recent price average — useful for seeing the general direction of the trend.

The Three Selectors

  • Crypto — switch between Bitcoin (BTC) and Ethereum (ETH).
  • Scenario — choose how the model interprets sentiment:
    • ⚪ Base — neutral, no bias. The model's honest best guess.
    • 🟢 Bullish — assumes positive sentiment is more meaningful. Shows the optimistic case.
    • 🔴 Bearish — assumes negative sentiment is more meaningful. Shows the cautious case.
  • Horizon — how far ahead to predict. 7 Days is the most reliable. 90 Days is more speculative — the band will be much wider.

The Market Mood Bar

The bar chart below the price chart shows the Fear & Greed Index over the last 90 days. Red bars mean people were fearful (prices often bounce from here). Green bars mean people were greedy (sometimes a warning sign). Yellow is neutral.

What is RSI?

RSI (Relative Strength Index) is a number from 0–100 that measures how fast a price has been moving up or down recently. Think of it as a "temperature gauge" for momentum:

  • Below 30 → Oversold. The price has dropped hard and fast. Sellers may be exhausted, and a bounce is historically more likely — though not guaranteed.
  • Above 70 → Overbought. The price has risen hard and fast. Buyers may be overextended, and a pullback is historically more likely.
  • 30–70 → Neutral territory. No strong signal either way.

RSI doesn't tell you when a reversal will happen — just that conditions are ripe. An oversold coin can keep falling; an overbought one can keep rising. It's one clue among many.

What is MACD?

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) tracks momentum by comparing two moving averages of price — a faster one (12-day) and a slower one (26-day). The difference between them is the MACD line. There's also a "signal line" (a 9-day average of MACD) and a "histogram" (the gap between the two):

  • MACD histogram positive (▲) — the fast average is above the slow one. Recent momentum is upward — a bullish sign.
  • MACD histogram negative (▼) — the fast average is below the slow one. Recent momentum is downward — a bearish sign.
  • Histogram shrinking toward zero — momentum is fading; a trend change may be near.

In plain English: MACD answers "is the price speeding up or slowing down, and in which direction?" It's most useful for confirming a trend rather than predicting exact turning points.
